THE REACT
REACTION & RHYTHM
Watch the pattern flash 3 times, then tap cells AS they light up on the beat. Tests reaction time, rhythmic timing, and anticipation under pressure.
🏀 Game speed, tempo, clutch timing — Can you play at 120 BPM when the game is on the line? This is the difference between a player who reacts and one who anticipates.

THE RECALL
WORKING MEMORY
Numbered cells flash on a grid — memorize which cell has which number, then tap them back in order: 1, 2, 3, 4, 5. Scales from 5 to 14 cells.
🏀 Play execution, basketball IQ — Coach calls a play with 5 positions. Can you remember where everyone goes? The kid who runs the play right every time has elite working memory.

THE REFLEX
VISUAL PROCESSING
Multiple cells flash ALL AT ONCE — capture the whole picture in a split second, then find them all. Flash times shrink from 2.5s to 0.8s at Elite speed.
🏀 Court vision, fast break reads — Can you see all 5 defenders in a split second? The play develops in 0.8 seconds — you're driving the lane, where are your teammates?

THE REPLAY
SEQUENCE MEMORY
Watch cells light up one by one with unique tones — then replay the exact sequence in order. Each cell has its own sound, adding auditory encoding. Sequences scale from 5 to 25 steps.
🏀 Play recall, muscle memory — Your coach drills a 12-step play. Can you run it from memory? The players who master complex sequences are the ones who execute under pressure.

THE RITMO
CHANGE DETECTION
Watch an original pattern, then watch the echo — ONE cell is different. Find the change before time runs out. You have 4 beats. Patterns scale from 3 to 12 cells.
🏀 Reading defensive shifts — Defense rotated. Who moved? Where's the open man now? The best playmakers see what changed before anyone else on the court.
